# ChoreQuest — Product Design Document
**Working Title:** ChoreQuest → **Final name candidates: *Ashen Keep* or *The Settlement***
**Version:** 1.0 — Post-Apocalyptic Pivot
**Date:** 2026-05-28

---

## 1. Vision

ChoreQuest is a family game disguised as a survival sim. It turns the real-world effort of a household — chores, learning, maintenance, care — into the fuel for a living, breathing post-apocalyptic settlement. The family doesn't just play together; they govern together, and every decision they make changes the story they're building.

The core promise: **doing real things at home matters inside the game, and decisions inside the game reflect who your family actually is.**

---

## 2. Core Design Pillars

1. **Real effort, real consequence.** Chores are not buttons to click. They are missions that feed a shared world. Skipping them is felt.
2. **Shared stakes, individual identity.** The settlement belongs to everyone. But each person has a Role that shapes what they experience, what they're good at, and how the story sees them.
3. **Decisions that matter.** The Council is not a settings menu. It is where the family governs. Laws have trade-offs. Votes have weight. Choices are permanent and shape the story's trajectory.
4. **A story worth following.** The main narrative is hand-written, branching, and reactive to how the family plays. It is not a backdrop — it is the reason you care about the settlement.
5. **Mobile-first, always in the background.** The game lives in the margins of daily life. Notifications pull you in. Sessions are short but meaningful. Progress never stalls completely.

---

## 3. World & Setting

**The world ended 18 years ago.** Not in fire, but in cascade — a perfect storm of infrastructure collapse, supply chain failure, and a pandemic that rewrote society faster than governments could respond. Most cities are hollow. Some have become warlord territory. Others are slowly being reclaimed.

Your family's **Settlement** is one of the rebuilding efforts. Small, vulnerable, but alive. You're not surviving anymore — you're trying to build something that lasts. The tone is grounded and human: closer to *The Last of Us* or *Station Eleven* than *Mad Max*. There is violence, loss, and moral complexity — but also hope, community, and humour.

The player's home region shapes the aesthetic (selectable at setup: arid/desert, temperate/forests, coastal, urban/ruins, cold/northern). This is cosmetic but affects flavour text and some event types.

## 5. Resource System

### 5.1 Basic Resources (generated by chores)

| Resource | Symbol | Earned by | Real-world parallel |
|----------|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| Food | 🥫 | Cleanliness, family chores | Feeding, caring for home |
| Energy | ⚡ | Maintenance chores | Keeping things running |
| Knowledge | 📚 | Learning chores | Study, reading, skills |
| Money | 💵 | Special/work chores | Real-world effort value |

### 5.2 Advanced Resources (generated by buildings)

Advanced resources are produced idly by buildings and are used for higher-tier construction, units, research, and events.

| Resource | Symbol | Source Building | Used For |
|----------|---------|----------------|----------|
| Medicine | 💊 | Clinic | Events, unit health, morale |
| Steel | 🔩 | Workshop | Defense structures, tools |
| Fuel | ⛽ | Refinery | Vehicles, generators, expeditions |
| Ammunition | 🧨 | Armoury | Combat units, defense |
| Morale | 💜 | Community Hall | All resource multiplier |
| Intel | 🔍 | Signal Tower | Exploration unlocks, events forewarning |

### 5.3 Personal Credits

Each family member earns **Personal Credits (⭐)** individually when their chores are approved. These are NOT shared. They are spent:
- In the **Shop** on personal in-game cosmetics, boosts, and titles
- On **real-world rewards** (screen time, choose dinner, outing) set by parents in the web dashboard
- On **role-specific upgrades** (see Section 7)

**Morale Multiplier** applies at point-of-award:
- Morale ≥ 75 → ×1.2 on all resources
- Morale 50–74 → ×1.0
- Morale < 50 → ×0.8

## 6. Buildings & Settlement

### 6.1 Building Tiers

Buildings require Basic Resources to construct, then generate Advanced Resources passively. Each building has 5 levels (×2 cost, ×1.5 output per level).

**Tier 1 — Foundation** (require only Basic Resources to build)

| Building | Cost | Generates | Unlocks |
|----------|------|-----------|---------|
| Food Store | 50 🥫 | +3 🥫/hr (storage max ×2) | Prevents famine events |
| Generator | 50 ⚡ | +5 ⚡/hr | Powers higher tier buildings |
| Library | 50 📚 | +4 📚/hr | Research queue |
| Workshop | 40 💵 + 30 ⚡ | +3 🔩/hr | Tier 2 buildings |
| Clinic | 40 📚 + 30 🥫 | +3 💊/hr | Injury recovery, events |
| Watchtower | 30 🔩 | +2 🔍/hr, early warnings | Defense bonus |

**Tier 2 — Growth** (require Advanced Resources)

| Building | Cost | Generates | Unlocks |
|----------|------|-----------|---------|
| Refinery | 80 ⚡ + 50 🔩 | +4 ⛽/hr | Vehicles, long expeditions |
| Armoury | 60 🔩 + 40 💵 | +3 🧨/hr | Defense units |
| Community Hall | 50 💊 + 40 📚 | +4 💜/hr (slows morale decay) | Council laws, diplomacy |
| Signal Tower | 60 📚 + 40 ⚡ | +4 🔍/hr, event preview | Chapter 3+ story |
| Greenhouse | 80 🥫 + 40 🔩 | +5 🥫/hr, famine immunity | Long-term food security |
| Barracks | 60 🧨 + 40 🔩 | Unit training | Attack/defense missions |

**Tier 3 — Legacy** (endgame)

| Building | Cost | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Archive | 200 📚 + 100 🔍 | Unlocks final story chapters |
| Vault | 150 💵 + 100 🔩 | Prevents resource loss from catastrophic events |
| Embassy | 150 💜 + 100 🔍 | Full diplomacy system |
| The Shop | 100 💵 + 50 📚 | Personal credit exchange, real-world rewards |

### 6.2 Settlement View

The settlement is displayed as a **scrollable card list**, not a grid or map. Each building is a card with:
- A **static illustrated sprite** (changes with building level — 5 states per building)
- Building name and current level
- Resource generation rate (e.g. "+5 ⚡/hr")
- Current resource stockpile bar
- Build / Upgrade button with cost breakdown

Cards are grouped by tier (Foundation → Growth → Legacy) and sorted by construction date within each tier. Unbuilt buildings appear as greyed-out blueprint cards at the bottom of their tier group.

This approach scales naturally to any number of buildings, works well in portrait mobile layout, and allows rich per-building detail without the complexity of drag-and-drop placement.

Building slots are limited per tier (Foundation: 6, Growth: 4, Legacy: 3 — total 13 max, choose wisely). The family must prioritise. There is no "build everything" — this is a strategic constraint that sparks Council discussion.

## 7. Roles & Individual Experience

This is the core design that makes a shared game feel personal.

### 7.1 Available Roles

Roles are chosen at signup (age-agnostic). They can be changed once per month via Council vote.

| Role | Symbol | Chore Bonus | Building Affinity | Exploration Bonus | Passive |
|------|---------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Engineer** | 🔧 | +50% ⚡ from Maintenance | Workshop, Refinery, Generator | Finds more Scrap/Steel | -10% build cost |
| **Scout** | 🗺️ | +30% all from any chore | Watchtower | -20% travel time, +30% loot | First pick of exploration rewards |
| **Medic** | 💊 | +50% 📚 from Learning | Clinic | Can heal injuries mid-mission | +2 💜/day for household |
| **Trader** | 💵 | +50% 💵 from Special chores | Shop, Vault | Finds rare trade goods | Better exchange rates in Shop |
| **Sentinel** | 🧨 | +40% ⚡ from Maintenance | Armoury, Barracks | Better combat outcomes | Reduces raid damage |
| **Scholar** | 📖 | +60% 📚 from Learning | Library, Archive, Signal Tower | Unlocks lore events | Faster research |

### 7.2 The Dual-Currency Split

```
SHARED POOL ─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  Food, Energy, Knowledge, Money                          │
  Advanced Resources                                      │
  Buildings, Events, Story, Council                       │
                                                          │
PERSONAL CREDITS ─────────────────────────────────────────┤
  Earned per approved chore (role bonus applies)          │
  Personal skill upgrades                                 │
  Shop purchases (in-game + real-world)                   │
  Personal arc items                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 7.3 Personal Skill Trees

Each Role has a 12-node skill tree unlocked with Personal Credits. Skills amplify the Role's strengths or fill gaps. Example (Scout):

```
SCOUT TREE
├── Efficient Packing       (exploration finds +10%)
├── Light Footing           (travel time -10%)
├── Scavenger's Eye         (rare loot +15%)
│   └── The Collector       (chance to find unique items)
├── Shortcut Knowledge      (-20% travel time)
│   └── Trailblazer         (unlock new map locations)
└── Combat Ready            (+20% defense in ambushes)
    └── Survivalist          (can survive one fatal combat hit)
```

### 7.4 Personal Arc

The story's **main thread** is shared — the settlement's fate is decided together. But each Role has **personal chapter inserts** that appear at story milestones, written from that character's perspective. The same event can read entirely differently:

> *[Medic]* "You were the one who noticed it first — the cough that spread too fast. If you hadn't acted, we'd have lost half the settlement. The others will never know how close it was."

> *[Sentinel]* "You kept watch that night. Three of them at the fence. You didn't wake anyone. You handled it. Some things are better kept between you and the dark."

This makes the same story beat feel personal and earned based on what role you play and what chores you did that week.

### 7.5 Council Voting Weight

Each family member's **vote weight in Council** scales with their chore completion rate for the current week:

- 0–25% completion → 0.5× vote weight
- 26–75% → 1× vote weight
- 76–100% → 1.5× vote weight

This means doing your chores literally gives you more political power in the family's decisions. It's never punitive (you still vote), but it creates a natural incentive and models real civic concepts.

## 8. Exploration System

### 8.1 Location Types

| Type | Threat | Primary Loot | Story Relevance |
|------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Abandoned Suburbs | Low | Food, Medicine | Personal arc events |
| City Centre Ruins | Medium | Steel, Ammo, Intel | Main story beats |
| Research Facility | High | Knowledge, rare tech | Scholar arc, story unlocks |
| Military Outpost | High | Ammo, Fuel, Weapons | Sentinel arc, faction events |
| Natural Areas | Low | Food, Medicine | Medic arc, morale events |
| Underground Bunkers | Very High | All rarities | Late-game story, Tier 3 unlocks |

### 8.2 V1 — Timed Missions

Exploration missions take real time based on location threat level and distance:

| Threat | Base Duration | Reduced By |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Low | 2 hours | Scout role (-20%), Refinery upgrade (-10%) |
| Medium | 4 hours | Scout role (-20%), Vehicles (-20%) |
| High | 8 hours | Vehicles (-20%), Fuel availability |
| Very High | 16 hours | Only Tier 3 gear reduces |

Results are resolved on return. Only one active expedition per family (expandable with Barracks).

### 8.3 V2 — Real Distance (Phase 2)

Distance replaces time. Integration with HealthKit (iOS) / Health Connect (Android):

- Low threat: 2 km walked
- Medium: 5 km
- High: 10 km
- Very High: 20 km (can be split across multiple family members)

Multiple family members can **pool their walking distance** for the same expedition. The Scout role counts steps at 1.3× rate. Families with young children get a "Caregiver Credit" system where pushing a pram counts.

The mission doesn't complete until the distance is walked. Distance is logged in background while the user goes about their day. This is the mechanic that gets people outside.

### 8.4 Exploration Events

Explorations are not passive. Mid-mission, decision events appear (timed — must respond within 4 hours):

**Examples:**
> *"Your scout finds a survivor. She claims she knows where a fuel cache is but needs food to guide you. Do you: [Share food -20 🥫] or [Leave her] or [Bring her back to settlement]?"*

> *"You find a building that's structurally sound. You can: [Loot it thoroughly — 8 more hours, more loot] or [Quick sweep — take what you can now] or [Mark it for the Settlement map — unlocks future mission]"*

> *"Ambush. You have ammo and a Sentinel in the party. [Fight — cost 30 🧨, chance of injury] or [Retreat — lose loot, no injury] or [Negotiate — cost 50 💵, guaranteed safe passage]"*

Outcomes feed into the hidden metric system (Section 11).

## 9. Random Events

Random events fire without user input, on a schedule weighted by how long since the last event and the Settlement's story chapter. They appear as push notifications.

### 9.1 Event Types

**Community Crisis** — the family must pool resources within a time limit:
> *"A harsh winter has arrived early. The Settlement needs 200 🥫 and 100 💊 within 48 hours or morale collapses."*

**Opportunity** — time-limited benefit:
> *"A passing trader group offers a supply drop in exchange for 150 💵. Accept within 24 hours."*

**Threat** — requires decision:
> *"Raiders have been spotted 3km out. The Watchtower detected them. [Fortify defences -50 🧨] or [Negotiate -100 💵] or [Prepare evacuation -200 🥫]"*

**Discovery** — story-relevant:
> *"Signal Tower picked up a transmission from Grid-7. It's faint and encrypted. Scout needed."*

**Consequence** (if previous event failed):
> *"The population is angry. Morale -20. A faction within the Settlement has begun questioning leadership."*

### 9.2 Failure State

Failing community crisis events does NOT end the game. Instead:
- Morale drops significantly
- A "discontent" hidden metric increases
- At high discontent, a Council "vote of no confidence" event fires — the family must pass a law or sacrifice resources to restore unity
- Repeated failures can trigger faction events and story branches

This models real consequence without being punishing. The game is never over.

## 10. Council System

The Council tab is where the family governs. It is not settings — it is the political heart of the game.

### 10.1 Laws

Laws are presented as cards. Each has a benefit and a cost. Passed laws are permanent unless repealed by a supermajority. Laws shape the story and unlock new content.

**Example Laws:**

| Law | Benefit | Cost | Metric Effect |
|-----|---------|------|---------------|
| Mandatory Study | +15% 📚 from all chores | Morale -5/week | +Scholar |
| Ration Protocol | Famine immunity | Food consumption -30%, morale -10 | +Pragmatic |
| Open Arms | Survivor recruitment speed +50% | Food drain +20% | +Empathic |
| Watch Programme | Raid warning time ×2 | Sentinel must be active | +Militaristic |
| Free Trade | Shop prices -20% | Intel sharing with other factions | +Trader |
| Isolation Protocol | No diplomacy penalties | No diplomacy benefits | -Empathic |
| Curfew | Morale stable during crisis | Children's chore options limited | +Pragmatic |

Laws accumulate. The constellation of passed laws affects what story chapters unlock and what faction events trigger.

### 10.2 Community Crisis Coordination

When a timed resource event is active, the Council tab shows a **crisis board** — a shared target with each family member's contribution tracked live. This is the primary co-op mechanic: visible progress toward a shared goal, each person contributing what they can.

### 10.3 Diplomacy

As the Signal Tower and Embassy are built, the settlement makes contact with **neighbouring factions** (written characters, not other game accounts — this is single-family):

- **Irongate Collective** — militaristic, offer protection for tribute
- **The Commons** — egalitarian traders, offer resources for knowledge
- **The Wardens** — scholarly survivalists, offer intel and research
- **Drift** — nomadic scavengers, unpredictable but information-rich
- **The Church of Before** — ideological, offer morale for ideological alignment

Diplomacy is managed through Council: offer trades, sign agreements, send ambassadors (uses exploration slot). Faction standing affects events and late-game story branches.

### 10.4 Family Rules (Parental Controls)

Parents (role-independent — whoever has the app's admin access) can set in the Council tab:
- Real-world reward catalogue (what Personal Credits can be redeemed for)
- Chore approval toggle (auto-approve or manual)
- Weekly credit caps
- Content sensitivity level (affects violence/darkness in narrative)

## 11. Hidden Metrics & Narrative Influence

Every significant decision — law passed, exploration event choice, Council vote, crisis response — contributes to five hidden metrics. These are never shown numerically to players. Their influence is felt, not read.

| Metric | What Raises It | Narrative Effect |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Militaristic** | Combat choices, defensive laws, Sentinel chores | More raid events (winnable), military faction options, darker story tone |
| **Scholar** | Knowledge chores, study laws, Research Facility explorations | Research breakthroughs, academic faction access, optimistic story tone |
| **Empathic** | Helping survivors, Open Arms law, Medic choices | Survivor recruitment events, morale boosts, humanist story beats |
| **Explorer** | Frequent explorations, Scout chores, new location unlocks | New map areas open sooner, unique expedition events |
| **Pragmatic** | Resource-maximising decisions, Ration laws, Trading | Better resource events, fewer morale crises, utilitarian story beats |

The metrics are not a morality system. There is no "good" or "evil." The story adapts to reflect who your family genuinely is as players — a militaristic family and a scholarly family play the same chapters but experience them very differently.

**Example:** Chapter 3 involves contact with a hostile faction. A Militaristic family gets a confrontation arc. A Diplomatic family gets a negotiation arc. A Scholarly family gets an intelligence arc. The outcome (faction neutralised) is the same. The *meaning* of it is different.

## 12. Story System

### 12.1 Structure

The main story is told across **6 Acts**, each unlocking when the settlement meets threshold conditions. Each Act contains 4–6 narrative events, some linear, some branching.

| Act | Title | Unlock Condition | Theme |
|-----|-------|-----------------|-------|
| 1 | **Kindling** | Auto (start) | Survival, who we are |
| 2 | **First Walls** | 3 buildings built, 500 resources | Community formation, first contact |
| 3 | **The Signal** | Signal Tower built, 10 explorations | Outside world, revelation |
| 4 | **Fractures** | Faction ally + enemy established | Internal conflict, ideology |
| 5 | **The Reckoning** | All Tier 2 buildings, 25 explorations | Major moral choice with lasting consequence |
| 6 | **What We Became** | All Tier 3 buildings, all metrics above threshold | Epilogue — shaped entirely by accumulated choices |

### 12.2 Story Events

Story events are presented as full-screen narrative cards with illustrated headers (simple flat art). Text is short and punchy. Choices are presented where relevant. Every choice is permanent.

**Example — Act 2, Event 3 "The Strangers at the Gate":**

> *Three people arrive at dawn. They're in bad shape — a man, a woman, and a child. They have skills. They have supplies. But your Intel is thin, and not everyone in the settlement trusts newcomers.*
>
> *Your Medic takes one look at the child and makes the call before you can stop her. "They're sick but not contagious. They need food and rest."*
>
> *What does the Council decide?*
>
> **[Take them in]** → +3 population, +Empathic, future survivor recruitment unlocked
> **[Quarantine first]** → +3 population (delayed), neutral metrics
> **[Turn them away]** → -Empathic, morale split in settlement, story reference in Act 4

Note: if the player has a Medic in the family, that character's name replaces "Your Medic" in the text — personalisation without requiring AI.

### 12.3 Branching & Permanence

Choices are permanent. The game does not let you undo story decisions. Late-game events reference early-game choices explicitly:

> *"The strangers you turned away in the winter of Year 2 are running Irongate now. They remember."*

This is what makes the story worth following — your family's history is baked into it.

## 13. Shop & Rewards

The Shop building unlocks once Tier 2 is reached. It has two sections:

### 13.1 Personal Credit Shop (Individual ⭐)

**In-game cosmetics & boosts:**
- Character skins / outfit for your avatar
- Role title upgrades ("Scout" → "Pathfinder" → "Ghost")
- Settlement cosmetics (flag, gate decal, entrance sign)
- XP boosts (double credits for next 3 chores)
- "Skip timer" tokens (reduce one exploration by 1 hour)

**Real-world rewards** (set by parents in web dashboard):
- 30 min extra screen time
- Choose tonight's dinner
- Skip one chore (once per week, parent sets this)
- Family movie night pick
- Earn a small cash allowance
- Custom entries (parents write their own)

### 13.2 Settlement Resource Exchange (Shared)

The Shop also functions as a trade post — the family can convert resources at less-than-ideal rates (simulating real trade inefficiency):
- 3 🥫 → 1 ⚡
- 2 📚 → 1 💊
- 5 💵 → any 2 basic resources

Exchange rates improve with the Trader role and Vault building.

### 13.3 Faction Trade

Once Diplomacy is established, factions offer periodic trade windows (visible in Council tab). These often have unique resources or story items not available elsewhere.

## 14. Engagement Design — The Retention Loop

The game is designed to stay relevant across the entire week:

| Cadence | Touchpoint |
|---------|-----------|
| **Daily** | Chore notifications, resource check, random event response |
| **Weekly** | Council law vote, new chore cycle, expedition return |
| **Monthly** | New story event unlocked, faction update, role change window |
| **Milestone** | Building complete, new Act, permanent story decision |

**The game never pauses.** If the family stops doing chores, morale decays, buildings underperform, and eventually a "discontent event" fires in Council. The game nudges rather than punishes.

---

## 15. Mobile-First Design Principles

- **Sessions under 3 minutes.** Check resources, launch expedition, claim a chore. Deep engagement is opt-in.
- **Notifications are meaningful.** Every push notification should represent something real: a crisis to respond to, a return from expedition, a Council vote needed, a story event available. No spam.
- **No internet, no problem.** Offline mode queues chore completions. Resource generation is calculated server-side on next sync.
- **Web app is parent-facing.** The web dashboard is for creating/managing chores, setting rewards, viewing analytics, and approving from a desktop. The game is played entirely on mobile.
- **Accessibility.** All text meets WCAG AA contrast. Core gameplay works without sound. Mixed-age families need readable font sizes.

## 17. Design Decisions — Resolved

1. **Population system:** ✅ **Yes.** Rescued survivors have simple persistent AI behaviour — they consume food passively, provide a small bonus tied to their implied skill (a former doctor reduces Medicine cost; a mechanic speeds builds), and can be **lost in events**. Their loss is felt. This raises the stakes of every Empathic decision and means the settlement has real continuity — faces the family remembers.

2. **Inter-family competition:** ✅ **No external competition.** Instead, a **within-family leaderboard** in the Council tab tracks each family member's contribution metrics: chores completed, resources contributed, explorations run, Council votes cast. This is celebratory not competitive — it surfaces who carried a tough week and gives everyone visibility into each other's effort. It also feeds the vote-weight system naturally.

3. **Story pacing:** ✅ **Hybrid — real-time floor + engagement ceiling.** Each Act has a minimum real-world time gate (Act 2: 1 week, Act 3: 3 weeks, Act 4: 6 weeks, Acts 5–6: 10 weeks+) AND an engagement requirement (buildings, explorations, chore milestones). Highly engaged families hit the engagement gates first and wait for the time gate. Low-engagement families hit the time gate first and wait for content gates. Neither group is locked out or rushed — the story feels earned by both.

4. **Content tone:** ✅ **No gore. No explicit depiction.** Violence is consequence, not spectacle — it happens off-screen or in implication. Death of adults, children, or pets can occur in narrative but is handled with restraint: referenced in past tense, mourned not described. The goal is emotional weight without trauma. Think *A Monster Calls* or *The Road* in tone — sorrow is present but purposeful.

5. **App name:** ✅ **Final candidates: *Ashen Keep* or *The Settlement*.** Decision deferred to pre-launch branding. Both work. *Ashen Keep* has stronger brand distinctiveness; *The Settlement* is more universally descriptive. Recommend deciding before building the icon/splash.
